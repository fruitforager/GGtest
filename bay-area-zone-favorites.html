Bay Area Fruit Favorites by Climate Zone

The following lists come from a poll of Golden Gate Chapter members, who were asked to recommend their favorite fruits and varieties.

Since the Golden Gate Chapter spans three distinct climate zones, there are three separate lists — find yours below.

🌦️ Find Your Climate Zone

The Bay Area's microclimates vary dramatically over short distances. Pick the zone that best matches where you garden — your chill hours and summer heat will shape almost every fruit decision you make.

Zone 1

🌫️ Fog Zone

‎ ‎ ‎❄️‎ ‎ 100–400 Chill hours

Cool summers. Mild winters.

📍 Heavy ocean influence. Includes Western San Francisco, Daly City, Pacifica, Half Moon Bay, Emeryville/Berkeley, Bolinas/Stinson Beach, Sausalito, El Cerrito, Richmond, Alameda.

The marine layer dominates — foggy mornings that linger, rarely much heat. Winters stay frost-light and mild. Chill hours are low at 100–400 hrs. Most standard deciduous fruit that needs cold winters to set fruit won't perform here. Focus on low-chill and subtropical evergreen fruiting plants that ripen in cooler temperatures without much summer heat.

✦ Members love:
Asian pears, Bearss lime, Eureka lemon, feijoas, figs ('Desert King', 'White Ischia'), loquats, passion fruit.

Zone 2

🌊 Facing the Bay

‎ ‎ ❄️‎ ‎ 400–900 Chill hours

Warm summers. Mild winters.

📍 Includes Berkeley, Oakland, Richmond, El Cerrito, Hayward, San Leandro, San Mateo, San Rafael, Novato, Martinez, Fremont, Vallejo.

More sun than the coast but still tempered by Bay breezes — summers are warm without being brutal, winters are mild but real. Chill hours run 400–900, enough for the vast majority of deciduous fruits without demanding the deep cold inland winters bring. This zone gives chapter members the broadest selection of anything they want to grow.

✦ Members love:
apples, Asian pears, figs, Meyer lemon, persimmons, plums, pluots, pomegranates

Zone 3

☀️ Inland

‎ ‎ ‎❄️‎‎ ‎ 600–1,500 Chill hours

Hot summers. Mild winters.

📍 Includes Concord, Walnut Creek, Lafayette, Danville, San Ramon, Pleasanton, Pittsburg, Antioch, Brentwood, Sunol, Dublin, Fairfield.

Real winters and real summers — the Bay's moderating influence barely reaches here. Chill hours run 600–1,500, the highest in the chapter, meaning standard and high-chill deciduous varieties that struggle on the coast genuinely thrive. Cold winters can damage frost-sensitive subtropicals requiring protection. Summer heat fully ripens all heat-loving fruits, and the long warm season lets growers push into varieties coastal members simply can't attempt.

✦ Members love:
apricots, cherries, figs, grapes, jujubes, peaches, persimmons, pomegranates
