Fruit Favorites
by Climate Zone

The following lists come from a poll of Golden Gate Chapter members, who were asked to recommend their favorite fruits and varieties.

Since the Golden Gate Chapter spans three distinct climate zones, there are three separate lists — find yours below.

🌦️ Find Your Microclimate Zone

The Bay Area's microclimates vary dramatically over short distances. Pick the zone that best matches where you garden — your chill hours and summer heat will shape almost every fruit decision you make.

Zone 1

🌫️ Fog Zone

‎‎ ‎❄️‎ ‎ 100–400 Chill hours

Cool summers. Mild winters.

📍 Cool Coastal. Cool summers, mild winters, heavy fog, and wind. Heavy ocean influence. Includes Western San Francisco, western Marin, Daly City, Pacifica, Half Moon Bay, Emeryville/Berkeley, Bolinas/Stinson Beach, San Mateo, Sausalito, El Cerrito, Richmond, Alameda.

Sunset Zone: Primarily Zone 17, with parts of Zone 16.

The marine layer dominates — cool summers, mild winters, heavy morning fogs that linger, wind, and rarely much heat. Winters stay frost-light and mild. Chill hours are low at 100–400 hrs. Most standard deciduous fruit that need cold winters to set fruit won't perform here. Focus on low-chill and subtropical evergreen fruiting plants that ripen in cooler temperatures without much summer heat.

Fruit Suitability: Limited to low-chill (<400 hours) and early-ripening varieties. Subtropicals like citrus, cherimoya, white sapote and avocados thrive here, while many temperate fruits (apples, cherries) struggle to mature or lack sufficient chill.

✦ Members love:
Asian pears, Bearss lime, Eureka lemon, feijoas, figs ('Desert King', 'White Ischia'), loquats, passion fruit.

Zone 2

🌊 Facing the Bay

‎ ❄️‎ ‎ 400–900 Chill hours

Warm summers. Mild winters.

📍 Includes Berkeley, Oakland, Albany, Richmond, El Cerrito, Hayward (western), San Leandro, San Mateo, Burlingame, Millbrae, San Rafael, Novato, central Marin, Martinez, Fremont, Vallejo.

Sunset Zone: Primarily Zone 16, transitioning to Zone 15 in colder pockets.

Moderate summers and winters with more sun than the coast but still tempered by Bay breezes — summers are warm without being brutal, winters are mild but real. Chill hours run 400–900, enough for the vast majority of deciduous fruits without demanding the deep cold inland winters bring. With less fog than the coast but more marine influence than inland, this zone gives chapter members the broadest selection of anything they want to grow.

Fruit Suitability: Supports low-to-moderate-chill varieties (200–600 hours). A wide range of temperate fruits (plums, peaches, apricots, apples) and subtropicals can succeed with proper variety selection.

✦ Members love:
apples, Asian pears, figs, Meyer lemon, persimmons, plums, pluots, pomegranates

Zone 3

☀️ Inland

‎ ‎‎❄️‎‎ ‎ 600–1,500 Chill hours

Hot summers. Mild winters.

📍 Includes Concord, Walnut Creek, Lafayette, Danville, Livermore, San Ramon, Pleasanton, Pittsburg, Antioch, Brentwood, Sunol, Dublin, Fairfield, Napa Valley and eastern Contra Costa/Alameda.

Sunset Zone: Primarily Zone 14 and Zone 15, with pockets of Zone 7 in higher elevations.

Real winters and real summers — the Bay's moderating influence barely reaches here. 600–1,500, the highest chill hours in the chapter, means standard and high-chill deciduous varieties that struggle on the coast can genuinely thrive here. Cold winters can damage frost-sensitive subtropicals requiring protection. Summer heat accumulation fully rips all heat-loving fruits, and the long warm season lets growers push into varieties coastal members simply can't attempt.

Fruit Suitability: Supports moderate-to-high-chill varieties (500–1,000+ hours). Ideal for traditional temperate fruits like cherries, apples, pears, jujubes and walnuts, as well as heat-loving varieties of grapes and stone fruit.

✦ Members love:
apricots, cherries, figs, grapes, jujubes, peaches, persimmons, pomegranates
