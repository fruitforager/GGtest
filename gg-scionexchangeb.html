🎒 What to Bring

  • Scion wish list (mind your local chill hours)
  • Gallon zip-top bags marked with your name
  • Painters tape + permanent marker
  • Notebook, pen, and handled tote
  • Scions/plants to share — properly labeled, drop off at Intake Station
  • Grafting tools to sharpen (small fee)
  • Cash, Credit, Debit, Apple Pay, or Google Pay

🚫 What Not to Bring

  • NO citrus or relatives — find out why
  • NO potted fig plants (dormant cuttings are fine)
  • NO diseased or pest-infected material
  • NO excess container leaf litter or heavy root soil
  • NO old wood with fruit spurs — bring last season's tip growth
  • NO non-edible plants or currently patented varieties

🌳 About Our Exchange

As the largest amateur fruit-growing organization in the world, our annual Scion Exchanges are the most prominent event by which California Rare Fruit Growers serves its mission. Find the best varieties for growing your own fruit.

We have a limited number of scions — availability depends on donations. In the spirit of the exchange, we invite you to harvest scions from your own trees to share. Even one or two trees is enough. Rare varieties are especially interesting, but beginning growers would love to try a 'Santa Rosa' plum or 'Fuji' apple.
