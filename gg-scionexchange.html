🎒What to Bring

  • Scion wish list (mind your local chill hours)
  • Gallon zip-top bags marked with your name
  • Painters tape + permanent marker
  • Notebook, pen, and handled tote
  • Scions/plants to share — properly labeled, drop off at Intake Station
  • Grafting tools to sharpen (small fee)
  • Cash, Credit, Debit, Apple Pay, or Google Pay

🚫What Not to Bring

  • NO citrus or relatives — find out why
  • NO potted fig plants (dormant cuttings are fine)
  • NO diseased or pest-infected material
  • NO excess container leaf litter or heavy root soil
  • NO old wood with fruit spurs — bring only last season's tip growth
  • NO non-edible plants
  • NO current patented plant varieties

🌳 About Our Exchange

As part of the largest amateur fruit-growing organization in the world, California Rare Fruit Growers’ annual Scion Exchanges are one of the clearest expressions of one of our missions: preserving and sharing exceptional fruit varieties.

At our Exchange, you’ll join other growers in trading dormant scionwood—cuttings used to graft new fruit trees. Together, you help create a living network of shared genetics across generations of orchards.

Scion availability depends entirely on community donations. We encourage you to bring scions from your own trees to share, whether that’s just a few branches or a diverse backyard collection. Rare varieties are always welcome, but so are familiar favorites like ‘Santa Rosa’ plum or ‘Fuji’ apple—especially if you’re just getting started with grafting. Just be sure you label what it is.

Each Exchange becomes a collaborative living archive, shaped by everyone who takes part.
