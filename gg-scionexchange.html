🎒 What to Bring

  1. Scion wish list — based on tastings, research, your chill hours, pollination, and rootstock compatibility.
  2. Gallon zip-top plastic bags to keep scions fresh. Include your name in case you set it down.
  3. Painters or masking tape to label your scions.
  4. Permanent marker to write on your tape.
  5. Notebook & pen to take notes.
  6. A handled tote to carry everything (with your name & number on it).
  7. Scions and plants to share — divisions, tubers, bare-root plants, seeds of unusual fruits, berries, nuts, vegetables, and herbs. Everything must be labeled. Drop off at the Scion Intake Station near the Welcome Table.
  8. Grafting knives and pruners to sharpen for a fee.
  9. We accept cash, credit/debit cards, Apple Pay, and Google Pay.
Do Not Bring
  • NO citrus or relatives (plants or scions/cuttings) — find out why
  • NO potted fig plants (cuttings are ok)
  • NO diseased or pest-infected plants
  • NO leaf litter from potted plants, or excess soil from roots/tubers
  • NO old wood with fruit spurs (two years or older) — bring only last year's tip growth. See photo
  • NO non-edible plants
  • NO currently patented plant varieties

📚 Scion Exchange Essential Knowledge

What is a Scion?

In a well-pruned orchard, last year's growth (scions) can be 3 feet or longer. Scions for grafting are harvested during the dormant season — clean, one-year-old wood about ¼ inch in diameter (no thinner than a pencil). Also known as fruitwood or budwood, scion wood ensures you get exactly the cultivar you want.

Cuttings to Root

Cuttings are propagated by rooting in soil — no grafting required. This includes figs, grapes, pomegranates, kiwis, mulberries, brambles, vines, and more.

🌳 About the Exchange

As the largest amateur fruit-growing organization in the world, our famous annual Scion Exchanges are the most prominent event by which California Rare Fruit Growers serves its mission. Find the best varieties for growing your own fruit.

We have a limited number of scions — availability depends on what is donated. In the spirit of the exchange, we invite you to harvest scions from your own trees and bring them to share. Even one or two trees is enough. Rare varieties are especially interesting, but beginning growers would love to try their hand at grafting a 'Santa Rosa' plum or 'Fuji' apple. Don't assume someone else will bring what you have. Leftover scions are saved for the next exchange.
