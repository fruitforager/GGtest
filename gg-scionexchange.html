🌱 Beginners Welcome! We all start somewhere. Bringing scions is not required to attend.

🎒 What to Bring

  • Scion wish list (mind your local chill hours).
  • Gallon zip-top plastic bags marked with your name.
  • Painters or masking tape + Permanent marker.
  • Notebook, pen, and handled carrying tote.
  • Scions/plants to share: Properly labeled, dropped at Intake.
  • Grafting tools to sharpen for a small fee.
  • We accept Cash, Credit, Debit, Apple Pay, and Google Pay.

🚫 Do Not Bring

  • NO citrus or relatives (find out why).
  • NO potted fig plants (dormant cuttings are totally fine).
  • NO diseased or pest-infected materials.
  • NO excess container leaf litter or heavy root soil.
  • NO old wood with fruit spurs (bring last season's tip growth).
  • NO non-edible plants or currently patented varieties.

🌳 About Our Exchange

As the largest amateur fruit-growing organization in the world, our famous annual Scion Exchanges are the most prominent event by which California Rare Fruit Growers serves its mission. Find the best varieties for growing your own fruit.

We have a limited number of scions — availability depends on what is donated. In the spirit of the exchange, we invite you to harvest scions from your own trees and bring them to share. Even one or two trees is enough. Rare varieties are especially interesting, but beginning growers would love to try their hand at grafting a 'Santa Rosa' plum or 'Fuji' apple.

Preserving Our Past. Planting Our Future.
